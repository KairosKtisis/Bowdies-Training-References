Bowdie's Old Fashioned
Small Rocks Glass  ·  Muddle + Stir
CocktailSpirit ForwardCitrusStirred
Build
Sell It
The house take on a classic — Buffalo Trace muddled with rum-soaked cherries, orange, and bitters over a large cube. Familiar enough to be a safe recommendation, distinct enough to be memorable.
Staff Notes
Buffalo Trace brings vanilla and caramel; the rum-infused cherries add a subtle sweetness that sets this apart from a standard Old Fashioned. The brûléed orange garnish adds a faint smokiness on the nose. Lead with this for guests who want something spirit-forward but approachable.
Ingredients
  • 2 oz Buffalo Trace
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 3 Rum-infused Cherries
  • 1 Orange Slice
  • 3 dash Angostura Bitters
Method
  1. Muddle orange slice, sugar, cherries, and bitters in Small Rocks Glass
  2. Add 2 oz Buffalo Trace
  3. Add Large Cube and stir
Garnish: Brûléed Orange
Pairs With
Kansas City, Bone Marrow, Prime Tartare

Build
Sell It
A New Orleans classic split between Sazerac rye and Pierre Ferrand cognac, tied together with Bénédictine and two bitters. Complex, layered, and meant for the guest who wants to be impressed.
Staff Notes
One of the most historically significant cocktails on the list — born at the Hotel Monteleone in New Orleans. The split base gives it both the spice of rye and the richness of cognac, with Bénédictine adding herbal depth. If a guest asks what's interesting, this is your answer.
Ingredients
  • 2 oz Sazerac Rye (100 Proof)
  • 1 oz Pierre Ferrand Cognac
  • ½ oz Contratto
  • 1 tbsp Dom Bénédictine
  • 3 dash Angostura Bitters
  • 3 dash Peychaud's Bitters
Method
  1. Combine all in mixing glass
  2. Add ice and stir
  3. Strain into Large Rocks Glass with Large Cube
Garnish: Skewer of Rum-infused Cherries
Pairs With
Bone-In Filet, Bone Marrow, Prime Tartare
Whiskey
Sazerac
Small Rocks Glass  ·  Mixing Glass + Strain
CocktailSpirit ForwardClassicStirred
Build
Sell It
The original American cocktail. Sazerac rye, Peychaud's, and an absinthe-rinsed glass — nothing unnecessary, nothing missing.
Staff Notes
The absinthe rinse is the detail that defines it — just enough anise on the nose to set the stage without dominating the rye. Peychaud's bitters are lighter and more floral than Angostura, giving the Sazerac its distinctive pink hue and subtle cherry note. For the guest who takes their whiskey seriously.
Ingredients
  • 2 oz Sazerac Rye
  • 1 tsp Sugar
  • 5 dash Peychaud's Bitters
  • ¼ oz Absente
Method
  1. Add ice to Small Rocks Glass immediately — set aside
  2. In mixing glass: add sugar, Peychaud's, rye, and Absente
  3. Add ice and stir until cold
  4. Empty ice from Rocks Glass
  5. Strain mixing glass contents into Rocks Glass
Garnish: Lemon Peel
Pairs With
Prime Tartare, Escargot, Bone Marrow

Gin, cognac, Lillet, lemon, and an absinthe-rinsed glass. A pre-Prohibition classic that earns its name — equal parts elegant and reviving.

The balance here is precise — Warner's gin, Pierre Ferrand cognac, and Lillet in equal measure with just enough lemon to lift it. The absinthe rinse is the ghost in the background, adding anise on the nose without touching the palate. A cocktail for guests who appreciate craft and history.

  4. Strain shaker into rinsed Martini Glass

Details
Description
Choux-based beignets — butter, water, flour, eggs, sugar, and salt cooked into a dough and fried. The key step is vigorous mixing over heat to build gluten before the eggs go in, which produces the fluffy, hollow interior. Light and airy in contrast to the richer desserts on the menu.
Kitchen Notes
Contains gluten, dairy, eggs. Vegetarian. The dough is prepped in advance and held — fried to order. The choux method (panade) is the differentiator from a standard doughnut: the steam from the water creates the rise, not leavening. A lighter finish for guests who want something sweet without the density of the brownie or cheesecake.
Ingredients
  • 1 lb Butter
  • 1 qt Water
  • 1 qt AP flour
  • 18 Eggs
  • 1/2 cup Sugar
  • Pinch Salt
Method
  1. Bring butter and water to a roaring boil in stockpot.
  2. Add flour and mix thoroughly into dough.
  3. Keep over heat (on and off) while mixing vigorously with rubber spatula — this builds gluten and is the most important step for fluffy beignets.
  4. Transfer dough to stand mixer on slow. Add salt and sugar. Add eggs one at a time.
  5. Place into proper container, label, date, initials.
